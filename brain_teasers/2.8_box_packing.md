Can you pack 53 bricks of dimensions 1x1x4 into a 6 x 6 x 6 box?

solution: 

This is a nice problem extended from a popular chess board problem. In that
problem, you have a 8 x 8 chess board with two small squares at the opposite diagonal
comers removed. You have many bricks with dimension 1 x 2. Can you pack 31 bricks
into the remaining 62 squares? (An alternative question is whether you can cover all 62
squares using bricks without any bricks overlapping with each other or sticking out of
the board, which requires a similar analysis.)
```
8 * 8 = 64, and 32 is Black and 32 is white

if we remove the opposite diagonal: they are the same color!

so 32 black and 30 white

one brick 1 x 2 is using 1 B and 1 W, so 31 bricks cannot fit 30 white , so the answer is not possible. 
```

theoritical: `6 * 6 * 6 = 36 * 6 = 36 * + 180 = 216` vs `53 * 4 = 212`

we can leverage the similar thinking: imaging 6 * 6 * 6 is full of 2 * 2 * 2 cubes: it will b  3^3 = 27 cubes. 

Let's color the cubes to black and white alternatively: 13 black 2x2 （layer by layer 5, 4, 5） and 14 white 2x2.  

So every brick of 1x1x4 will take 2 1x1 black and 2 1x1 white, nomatter how the position is. then 53 brisks will take 53*2 = 106 black and 106 white

How many black 1x1 cube  do we have: 13*4 = 52, so no space for 53 bricks.
